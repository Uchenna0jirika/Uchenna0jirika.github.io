Double Barrel Case Study

Enhancing the user experience for football statistics

OverView

The original Double Barrel site suffered from a confusing user experience and missed critical sales opportunities. The navigation was limited and content was unclear, leaving potential customers with questions and potential frustration.

Problem

The Double Barrel Metal Works website was failing as a business tool. While it displayed their custom smokers, the site was fundamentally built to inform, not to sell. This was compounded by unclear content, weak calls-to-action, and a visual design that failed to engage users or highlight the product's quality, ultimately damaging conversion rates and leaving sales on the table.

Solution

I led a strategic redesign to transform the site into a powerful sales engine. The solution focused on creating a clear, compelling user journey from discovery to getting users to inquire about getting their own custom smoker. This included a new hero section with a strong value proposition, high-impact visuals that showcased the product craftsmanship, and a streamlined, intuitive flow. The new design not only tells a stronger brand story but also directly facilitates sales, turning visitor interest into confirmed revenue.

Discovery

Research

Problem: Secondary research confirmed that poor aesthetics destroy credibility, unclear hero sections increase bounce rates, and inefficient checkouts directly limit revenue potential.

  • Solution:
  • I translated these insights into a user-centric design:
  • Building trust with a visually compelling hero section.
  • Increasing revenue potential by streamlining the checkout flow.
  • Enhancing engagement by rewriting product copy to focus on storytelling and user benefit.

    • Persona

    I synthesized secondary research into actionable insights by identifying key user pain points and patterns. From these findings, I created a persona that captured the target audience’s needs and behaviors, ensuring a clear understanding of user perspectives and motivations.

    Ideation

    Problem Statements (HMW)

    To deepen my understanding of users' needs, I formulated How Might We statements. These statements transformed identified pain points into open-ended questions, guiding the ideation process toward potential solutions to address these challenges.

  • HMW improve upon current navigation (hamburger)(improve labels)(drop down)(embedded vertical)
  • HMW present content in a clear manner(improve grouping) (use images)(descriptive text)(display box method subtly)(labels)(hierarchy)
  • HMW add engagement through visual design(images of product)(visuals rather than still images)(contrast)(strong use of brand colors)(short paraphrases)(legible typography)(hierarchy)
  • HMW allow users to checkout or join waitlist with minimal fuss(one click method)(pricing transparency)(no distractions)(one page)(simple)(minimal info required)(safety measures displayed)(minimal input fields)(two column design)

    • Site Map

    I then translated user objectives into a concrete structure by creating the website's sitemap. This step ensured the redesigned experience would be built on a foundation of intuitive organization and seamless navigation, directly setting the stage for enhanced user satisfaction.

    Sketching

    • Foundation in User Flow: Transcribed prior research on user objectives and tasks into concrete screen layouts.

    • Rapid Iteration: Utilized sketching to quickly explore a range of design concepts and structural options.

    • Balanced Fidelity: Crafted sketches with enough detail to validate the user experience while remaining simple enough to encourage feedback and iteration.

    Wire Frames

    I evolved the sketched concepts into a full set of wireframes. This created a precise structural model of the entire user interface, prioritizing a logical layout and clear user pathways as the essential foundation for the final design.

    Design

    Style Guide

    I developed a cohesive visual system guided by color theory to enhance UX and strengthen brand recognition. This was paired with meticulously researched typography to ensure optimal legibility and a professional tone. Together, these elements create an immersive and trustworthy experience tailored to the data-driven needs of sports analysts and enthusiasts.

    Prototype

    I utilized Figma to create a prototype of my application, bringing its design to life for usability evaluation. The prototype enabled me to navigate through different sections, identifying and addressing any issues along the way. Experiencing the design as if it were a real application provided valuable insights into user interactions. This hands-on approach enhanced my understanding of functionality and guided improvements to enhance the overall user experience.
